Preparation Time: 1 hour (Total), 20 minutes (Preparation), 40 minutes (Cooking) Ingredients: 2 cups Ofada rice 1 onion, chopped 2 red bell peppers, chopped 2 tomatoes, chopped 2 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo), chopped 1 cup palm oil Assorted meats (beef, goat meat, tripe) 2 tablespoons ground crayfish 2 tablespoons locust beans (iru) Salt, to taste Instructions: Wash the Ofada rice and soak in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside. Blend the chopped onions, red bell peppers, tomatoes, and scotch bonnet peppers into a smooth paste. Heat palm oil in a pot, add the blended pepper mixture, and fry until the oil separates. Add the assorted meats and cook until browned. Stir in the ground crayfish and locust beans. Cook for another 5 minutes. Add the soaked Ofada rice to the pot and stir to combine with the sauce. Add water or stock to the pot, enough to cover the rice. Season with salt. Cover the pot and simmer for about 40 minutes or until the rice is cooked and fluffy. Serve the Ofada rice hot with assorted meats and stew on the side. Fun Fact: Ofada Rice, also known as unpolished or brown rice, is a traditional Nigerian rice variety named after the town Ofada in Ogun State. It is characterized by its unique aroma, nutty flavor, and slightly chewy texture.
