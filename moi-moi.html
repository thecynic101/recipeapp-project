Preparation Time: 2 hours 30 minutes (Total), 30 minutes (Preparation), 2 hours (Steaming) Ingredients: 2 cups peeled beans (black-eyed peas) 1 onion, chopped 2 red bell peppers, chopped 2 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo), chopped 1/2 cup vegetable oil 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth 2 eggs 1 teaspoon baking powder Salt, to taste Instructions: Rinse the peeled beans thoroughly and soak in water for at least 4 hours or overnight to soften. Drain the soaked beans and blend into a smooth paste along with chopped onions, red bell peppers, and scotch bonnet peppers. Transfer the blended mixture into a large bowl. Add vegetable oil, chicken or vegetable broth, beaten eggs, baking powder, and salt. Mix well to combine. Grease small pudding molds or ramekins with oil and fill them with the Moi Moi mixture. Place the filled molds in a large pot or steamer with boiling water. Cover and steam for about 2 hours until the Moi Moi is firm and fully cooked. Allow the Moi Moi to cool slightly before unmolding and serving. Serve Moi Moi warm as a side dish or snack, often accompanied by rice or bread. Fun Fact: Moi Moi is a traditional Nigerian dish made from ground beans and typically steamed until firm. It is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as a side dish, snack, or even a main course, and it is often served at celebrations and special occasions.
