Nigerian Beef Stew

Preparation Time: 1 hour 30 minutes (Total), 20 minutes (Preparation), 1 hour 10 minutes (Cooking)

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg beef, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 red bell peppers, chopped
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo), chopped
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions and minced garlic. Saut√© until onions are translucent.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes, red bell peppers, and scotch bonnet peppers to the pot. Cook until softened and the mixture forms a thick paste.
  4. Stir in tomato paste, thyme, curry powder, bay leaves, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 5 minutes to blend flavors.
  5. Add the beef pieces to the pot and stir to coat with the sauce. Cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the beef is browned on all sides.
  6. Pour in enough water to cover the beef, then bring the stew to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 1 hour until the beef is tender and the stew has thickened.
  7. Adjust seasoning if needed and remove bay leaves before serving.
  8. Serve the Nigerian Beef Stew hot with rice, yam, or any preferred side dish.

Fun Fact: Nigerian Beef Stew is a staple dish in Nigerian cuisine and is often served as a main course alongside rice, yam, or other starches. It is known for its rich and flavorful tomato-based sauce, which is packed with aromatic spices and vegetables.
