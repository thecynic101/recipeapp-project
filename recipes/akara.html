Akara

Preparation Time: 45 minutes (Total), 15 minutes (Preparation), 30 minutes (Cooking)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups peeled beans (black-eyed peas)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo), chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground crayfish (optional)
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the peeled beans thoroughly and soak in water for at least 4 hours or overnight to soften.
  2. Drain the soaked beans and blend into a smooth paste along with chopped onions and scotch bonnet peppers. Add a little water if needed to achieve a thick batter consistency.
  3. Transfer the blended mixture into a large bowl. Add ground crayfish (if using) and salt. Mix well to incorporate.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large skillet to 350°F (180°C).
  5. Using a spoon or your hands, scoop a portion of the bean batter and carefully drop it into the hot oil. Repeat to fry several Akara balls at a time, but do not overcrowd the pan.
  6. Fry the Akara balls for about 3-5 minutes on each side until golden brown and cooked through.
  7. Remove the fried Akara balls from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  8. Serve the Akara hot as a delicious and crispy Nigerian street food snack or breakfast item.

Fun Fact: Akara is a popular Nigerian street food made from deep-fried bean batter. It is often enjoyed as a snack or breakfast item, served with pap (akamu), bread, or eaten on its own.
