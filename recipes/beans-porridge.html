Beans Porridge

Preparation Time: 1 hour 30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups black-eyed beans
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 bell peppers (any color), chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground crayfish
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons palm oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the peeled beans thoroughly and soak in water for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked beans and transfer them to a pot. Add enough water to cover the beans and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and simmer the beans for about 45 minutes to 1 hour until tender.
  4. In a separate skillet, heat palm oil over medium heat.
  5. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and bell peppers to the skillet. Cook until softened.
  7. Once the beans are cooked, add the sautéed vegetables to the pot of beans.
  8. Stir in ground crayfish, ground cayenne pepper, and salt to taste. Mix well to combine.
  9. Continue to simmer the beans porridge for another 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the flavors meld together and the porridge thickens to the desired consistency.
  10. Remove from heat and serve the beans porridge hot as a hearty and nutritious meal.

Fun Fact: Beans porridge is a traditional Nigerian dish that is rich in protein and fiber. It is commonly enjoyed as a main course and is often served with a side of fried plantains or boiled yam. Beans porridge can be seasoned and customized with various vegetables and spices according to personal preference.
