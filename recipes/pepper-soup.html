Pepper Soup

Preparation Time:1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 500g assorted meats (such as goat meat, beef, or chicken), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo), chopped
  • 1 chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, peeled and minced
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 1 tablespoon ground uziza seeds (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon ground ehuru (calabash nutmeg) (optional)
  • 2 liters water or beef/chicken broth
  • Salt, to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley or scent leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the assorted meats, chopped onions, minced garlic, minced ginger, chopped scotch bonnet peppers, ground crayfish, ground uziza seeds (if using), and ground ehuru (if using).
  2. Pour in the water or broth, ensuring that the meats are fully submerged.
  3. Pour in the water or broth, ensuring that the meats are fully submerged.
  4. Place the pot over medium heat and bring the mixture to a boil.
  5. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let the soup simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meats are tender.
  6. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with salt if necessary.
  7. Serve the Pepper Soup hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley or scent leaves.
  8. Serve hot as a delicious side dish or main course.

Fun Fact: Pepper Soup is a spicy and flavorful Nigerian soup that is typically enjoyed as a appetizer or main course. It is known for its bold flavors and warming properties, making it a popular choice during cold weather or as a comfort food.
