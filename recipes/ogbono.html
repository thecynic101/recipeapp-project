Ogbono Soup

Preparation Time: Preparation Time: 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ground ogbono seeds (also known as African mango seeds)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cups assorted meat (such as beef, tripe, and cow feet), cut into bite-sized pieces.
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo), chopped
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • Assorted meats (beef, goat meat, tripe)
  • 1/2 cup dried fish, cleaned and deboned
  • 1/2 cup crayfish, ground
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cups chopped spinach or bitter leaf (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 tablespoons palm oil
  • 4 cups beef or chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Wash the Ofada rice and soak in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Blend the chopped onions, red bell peppers, tomatoes, and scotch bonnet peppers into a smooth paste.
  3. Heat palm oil in a pot, add the blended pepper mixture, and fry until the oil separates.
  4. Add the assorted meats and cook until browned.
  5. Stir in the ground crayfish and locust beans. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Add the soaked Ofada rice to the pot and stir to combine with the sauce.
  7. Add water or stock to the pot, enough to cover the rice. Season with salt.
  8. Cover the pot and simmer for about 40 minutes or until the rice is cooked and fluffy.
  9. Serve the Ofada rice hot with assorted meats and stew on the side.

Fun Fact: Ofada Rice, also known as unpolished or brown rice, is a traditional Nigerian rice variety named after the town Ofada in Ogun State. It is characterized by its unique aroma, nutty flavor, and slightly chewy texture.
